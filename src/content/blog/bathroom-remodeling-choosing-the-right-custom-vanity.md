## What size custom vanity do I need for my bathroom?

Your custom vanity should measure between 24 and 72 inches wide, with at least 30 inches of clearance space in front for comfortable movement. Single-sink vanities work well in the 24-48 inch range, while double-sink configurations require 60-72 inches to accommodate two users comfortably.

Start by measuring your available wall space and marking where plumbing connections exist. Account for door swings, toilet placement, and shower entry—you need clear paths for daily use. Double vanities transform shared bathrooms by eliminating morning bottlenecks, while a well-proportioned single sink creates an elegant focal point in powder rooms.

The height matters too. Standard vanity height sits at 32 inches, but "comfort height" vanities at 36 inches reduce back strain for taller homeowners. ## Which materials are best for custom vanities?

Solid hardwoods like oak, maple, and cherry deliver superior durability in bathroom environments, with proper moisture-resistant finishes extending lifespan by 15-20 years. Marine-grade plywood offers comparable strength at lower cost, while MDF works for painted finishes when sealed with polyurethane or waterborne varnish.

I've built vanities from every material imaginable, and hardwoods consistently outlast alternatives in humid conditions. Oak brings timeless grain patterns and natural moisture resistance. Maple provides a smooth, contemporary canvas for stains or paint. Cherry develops a rich patina that deepens with age—perfect for traditional designs.

Plywood construction with hardwood veneers gives you quality without breaking the budget. Look for furniture-grade plywood with at least seven layers. Avoid particleboard entirely; it swells and crumbles when exposed to bathroom humidity. Your finish matters as much as the base material. We apply three coats of waterborne polyurethane to every surface, including hidden areas where moisture sneaks in. ## What maintenance keeps my custom vanity looking new?

Maintain your custom vanity by wiping spills immediately, cleaning weekly with damp microfiber cloths, and avoiding harsh chemicals that strip protective finishes. Reapply furniture wax every 6 months to wood surfaces and tighten hardware annually. This routine preserves beauty and function for 20-30 years.

Good maintenance takes minutes but adds decades to vanity life. Water is wood's enemy—wipe up toothpaste splatters and water drops right away. For weekly cleaning, skip the spray bottles full of chemicals. A damp microfiber cloth removes 99% of bathroom grime without damaging finishes.

Deep clean monthly using mild soap and water, drying thoroughly afterward. Paste wax applied twice yearly adds moisture protection and restores luster to natural wood finishes. Check under your sink quarterly for leaks; catching a drip early prevents thousands in water damage. Tighten loose hardware and adjust door hinges as needed—usually once or twice yearly.

Avoid abrasive cleaners, bleach, and ammonia-based products that dull finishes and break down protective coatings. Use cutting boards or trays under styling tools; heat damages wood surfaces. Your bathroom fan matters too—run it during showers to reduce humidity that warps wood over time. Our team at firstclasswoodworks.com provides specific care instructions for your finish type, ensuring your vanity stays beautiful through daily use.
