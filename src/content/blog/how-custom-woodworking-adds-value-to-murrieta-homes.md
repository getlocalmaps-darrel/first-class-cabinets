## How does custom woodworking affect energy efficiency in homes?

Custom woodworking improves home energy efficiency by eliminating gaps and air infiltration points through precise measurements and professional installation that match your home's exact dimensions. Well-crafted wooden windows, doors, and built-ins with proper weather-stripping reduce drafts and thermal transfer, while quality hardwood itself provides better insulation values than hollow-core or particle board alternatives used in standard construction.

The U.S. Department of Energy reports that air leakage through poorly fitted doors, windows, and cabinetry accounts for 25-40% of heating and cooling costs. When we build custom work, we're measuring to 1/16" tolerances and scribing pieces to follow actual wall contours—not assuming everything is square and plumb like factory cabinets do.

Here's something most homeowners don't consider: the mass of solid hardwood provides thermal mass that moderates temperature swings. A room with substantial custom woodwork—built-in bookcases, wall paneling, or solid wood cabinetry—maintains more stable temperatures than rooms with minimal wood content. This thermal stability reduces HVAC cycling and energy consumption.

We also integrate energy-conscious details like toe-kick drawers that utilize otherwise-dead space, reducing the need for standalone storage furniture that blocks airflow.
