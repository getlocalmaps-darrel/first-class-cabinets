The real timeline killer isn't construction — it's waiting 2-3 weeks for permits from [Murrieta Building & Safety](https://www.murrietaca.gov/190/Building-Safety) and another 3-4 weeks for custom cabinet fabrication.

After completing over 200 kitchen remodels in Riverside County, I can tell you the timeline depends entirely on preparation. If we've ordered your Cambria quartz countertops and have cabinets in our shop before demolition day one, you're cooking in your new kitchen within five weeks. ## What Should You Look for in a Kitchen Remodeler?

Hire a kitchen remodeler in Murrieta with an active California contractor's license (verifiable through CSLB.ca.gov), a portfolio of 50+ local projects, and specific experience with your desired cabinet line and countertop material. Request references from projects completed in the past 12 months, verify workers' compensation insurance, and confirm they pull permits rather than working "under the table."

Look, I've been in this trade since 2009, and I can spot red flags immediately. Any contractor who says "we don't need permits for this" is setting you up for disaster. Murrieta requires permits for anything touching plumbing, electrical, or gas lines — period. When you sell your home, unpermitted work becomes your liability, potentially killing deals or requiring expensive retroactive permitting.

Here's my checklist after building 230+ kitchens across Southwest Riverside County:

**Experience That Matters:**
- Ask how many kitchens they've completed in Murrieta specifically. Local experience means they know which inspectors are strict about what, which suppliers deliver on time, and which HOAs require architectural review.
- Request photos of three projects similar in scope to yours. If they can't show you work that matches your vision, they're learning on your dime.
- Verify their license through [California State License Board](https://www.cslb.ca.gov/) — license #1103734 for us. Active status means bonded, insured, and accountable.

**Transparency in Process:**
- Demand itemized estimates breaking down cabinets, countertops, labor, permits, and contingency. "One price covers everything" quotes hide markups and prevent you from comparison shopping.
- Ask which brands they install regularly. We primarily spec Thomasville, Nieu, and KraftMaid because we've installed these lines for years and know their quality holds up. Contractors who say "we work with all brands" often lack deep expertise in any.

**Portfolio Evidence:**
- Check completed projects in person if possible. We're happy to connect clients with past customers in neighborhoods like [Temecula](https://www.firstclasswoodworks.com/cities/temecula/) or [Lake Elsinore](https://www.firstclasswoodworks.com/cities/lake-elsinore/) so you can see how cabinets hold up 3-5 years later.
